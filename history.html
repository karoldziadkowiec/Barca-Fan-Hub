History

History of the club

From the beginning

FC Barcelona is a football club with a long history, founded on November 29, 1899 by Joan Gamper and friends. The first president was Walter Wild, the team captain - Gamper. The club adopted the colors of claret and navy blue and the coat of arms of the city of Barcelona. The first match was played on 8 December 1899, losing 0-1 to England. The club strove to have its own stadium, which resulted in the first derby meeting against RCD Espanyol on the pitch at the Hotel Casanovas. The club participated in the Copa del Rey and reached the final in 1902, where they lost to Athletic Bilbao. Joan Gamper became president in 1908, which helped the club grow. Barça won the Spanish league and the Copa del Rey in the 1909/1910 season. In the 1920s and 1930s, the club developed, winning more titles. In the pre-war period and during the civil war, the club had difficulties, but thanks to commitment and determination it survived. After the war, political and sports changes took place. In the 1980s, FC Barcelona achieved successes, especially in the 1991/1992 season under the leadership of coach Johan Cruyff. Champions League Cup won. In 2003, Joan Laporta became the new president, and bringing in Ronald and changing the style of play to an offensive one brought success. In the 2005/2006 season, the club won the Spanish Championship and the Champions League Cup. In 2008, Josep Guardiola became a coach, and the period of his tenure was the golden era of FC Barcelona. The team has won many titles, including the Spanish Championship, Champions League and others. The history of the club is full of changes, successes and challenges that have contributed to shaping the identity of FC Barcelona as one of the most recognizable football clubs in the world.
